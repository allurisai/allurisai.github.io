Misinformation Detection with Social Behavior Analysis

Misinformation is not simply fake or real — it exists on a spectrum. I built a multi-class classification system trained on the LIAR dataset of 12,836 real fact-checked political statements from PolitiFact.com. The system classifies any statement into six credibility levels from True to Pants on Fire, while also analyzing social behavior patterns such as which venues, political parties, and job titles are most associated with false claims. The project includes a Flask web app where you paste any statement and instantly get a credibility prediction alongside social context insights.

Compared Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and fine-tuned BERT for six-class misinformation classification
Social behavior analysis to identify misinformation patterns across venue, party, and speaker role
Flask web app returns credibility level, confidence score, and social context for any input statement
